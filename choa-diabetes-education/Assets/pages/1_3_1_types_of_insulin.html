Types of Insulin

There are two types of insulin commonly used to treat diabetesâ€”rapid acting insulin and long acting insulin. Long acting insulin is given once a day at the same time each day. Rapid acting insulin is given with meals and to correct high blood sugars.

Long-acting insulin

Lantus / Levemir / Basaglar / Tresiba / Semglee

  • Works for approximately 24 hours to account for natural fluctuations in blood sugar throughout the day.
  • Important to take once a day at the same time each day, regardless of what your current blood sugar is.

Your diabetes doctor will prescribe a dose that remains the same each day unless your diabetes doctor tells you otherwise.

Rapid-acting insulin

Humalog / Novolog / Ademelog / Fiasp

  • Taken when carbohydrates are eaten to account for change in blood sugar as a result of carbohydrate consumption
  • Taken when blood sugars are elevated and correction dose is needed to bring blood sugar back in target range
