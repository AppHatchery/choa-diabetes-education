Everyone needs a healthy, balanced diet, whether we have diabetes or not

Type of food

You’ve learned in the previous section that rapid acting insulin is calculated based on what your blood sugar is pre-meal and how many carbs you plan to eat. To better understand how to calculate insulin based off carbohydrate consumption, we need to understand which foods have carbs and why they are important to our bodies.

  • Carbs—raise blood glucose, brain’s preferred source of fuel.
    • EXAMPLES: starches (bread, cereal, crackers, grains, rice, pasta), starchy vegetables (potatoes, corn, peas, beans), all fruit and fruit juices, milk, yogurt, sugary foods (candy, regular soda, jelly, syrup), sweets and desserts (cakes, cookies, pies, ice cream)
  • Fat—fat is also used for energy, with some fats healthier than others
    • EXAMPLES: oils, butter, salad dressings, nuts, seeds, mayonnaise, sour cream, cream cheese, bacon, sausage, olives, avocados
  • Protein—used to grow and repair cells in our body. Sometimes used for energy.
    • EXAMPLES: beef, pork, chicken, turkey, fish and seafood, eggs, cheese, nuts, seeds, and peanut butter
