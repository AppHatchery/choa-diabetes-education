How to calculate insulin dosages

Insulin for blood sugar

There are two ways insulin can be calculated to bring down a high blood sugar — correction factor/insulin sensitivity, or sliding scale. Your diabetes doctor will tell you which way is best for your family

Calculating correction dose based on correction formula and insulin sensitivity factor

  • Gives most precise dosing for high blood sugars
  • Use for high blood sugars. Child’s diabetes doctor will set the blood sugar level where correction dosing needs to be given
  • If pre-meal blood sugar(BS) is over 150, take the BS reading and subtract 100. Then Divide what is left by the correction factor, the result is the insulin needed to correct high BS

Example: for blood sugar over 150, use formula (BG-100)/50

  • 1) Blood sugar is 300
  • 2) (300-100) = 200
  • 3) 200/50 = 4 units. Child is to take 4 units IN ADDITION to meal dose for high blood sugar

Sliding scale dosing

  • Use for high blood sugars
  • Your diabetes doctor will tell you how much insulin to take for specific blood sugar ranges above target
