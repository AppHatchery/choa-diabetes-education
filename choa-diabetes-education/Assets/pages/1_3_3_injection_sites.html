Injection sites and rotation

You can give insulin shots in the abdomen, buttocks, arms, or thighs.

  • Change the spot each time you give a shot to keep your skin healthy.
  • Rotate spots within each area (like different parts of the tummy).
  • Using the same spot too often can cause lumps or scars, which can stop insulin from working properly.
  • Make a simple chart or plan to help remember where to give each shot.
      Example:
      • Right arm – breakfast
      • Left arm – lunch
      • Tummy – dinner
      • Buttock – bedtime
