When to call the doctor


Your diabetes team is at your fingertips to be used as a resource at all times. It is important to call the diabetes doctor for guidance anytime the following occurs:

  • When you have given glucagon
  • If ketones are moderate or large
  • If child is vomiting and cannot keep fluids down
  • Blood sugar has been low multiple times in one day
  • If 3 blood sugars are above 240 in a day
  • If long- acting insulin dose has been missed
  • If child is sick and blood sugars are consistently elevated.

For routine needs surrounding blood sugar pattern management, you can email the diabetes education team at CPGdiabetes@choa.org
